Note: a small number of source terms contain a mangled Unicode replacement character (U+FFFD, likely from an original em-dash or special character lost in an earlier encoding conversion) - e.g. "EPSTEIN�BARR VIRUS" and "HYPERTRIGLYCERIDEMIA�". These must be matched byte-for-byte (including the replacement character) when building the dict and appending new lines, or they will look unclassified forever. Read the CSV as UTF-8 and don't try to "fix" or strip these characters.

## The methodology rules established (apply these to the remaining terms)

1. **Conservative default**: only count a term as CVD/COPD/T2D/RMD if it names an actual, specific diagnosis - not a risk factor, not a vague symptom, not a lab value alone. This was Mohamed's explicit decision, confirmed in questions-for-mentor/2026-07-01.md and 2026-07-07.md.

2. **Risk factors and vague symptoms go to NEEDS_REVIEW (pending mentor), not straight to excluded**: specifically hypertension, high cholesterol/hyperlipidemia/hypertriglyceridemia, obesity, and metabolic syndrome (the composite of those). Every spelling variant and abbreviation of these terms found so far has been tagged NEEDS_REVIEW - keep doing this for any new variants found in the remaining terms.

3. **MSK pain/symptom-without-diagnosis also goes to NEEDS_REVIEW**: arthralgia, back pain, joint pain, neck pain, shoulder pain, hip pain, knee pain, decreased range of motion, muscle aches/myalgia - i.e. any joint or muscle-region symptom that doesn't name an actual diagnosed condition. This was extended from the original "chronic ankle pain" example in the mentor question, on the reasoning that it's the same category of problem (symptom vs. diagnosis) and RMD is exactly the category this affects. Exception: when the pain is explicitly attributed to an acute injury or surgery in the term itself (e.g. "neck pain (neck injury)," "back pain from melanoma surgery"), code it NOT_A_HEALTH_CONDITION instead, since it's clearly a transient post-injury/post-surgical symptom rather than the kind of chronic MSK complaint the pending mentor question is about.

4. **Diagnoses that ARE counted as RMD**: osteoarthritis, arthritis (plain, unqualified, including "facet arthritis" and site-specific arthritis like "knee arthritis"), gout (including all variants - gout is a specific inflammatory joint disease, not just a symptom), rheumatoid arthritis, herniated/bulged/compressed discs (including terms like "C6 disk hernia"), degenerative disc/joint disease, spinal stenosis (including "neck spinal stenosis"), radiculopathy/radiculitis/sciatica (nerve compression from disc disease), meniscus tears, tendinosis/tendinopathy (chronic, e.g. Achilles tendinosis, tennis/golfer's elbow), fibromyalgia, congenital joint dysplasia, arthropathy/arthrosis, ankylosing spondylitis, medial/lateral epicondylitis.

5. **Diagnoses NOT counted as RMD (localized/mechanical or acute, kept as OTHER_CONDITION_NOT_TRACKED or NOT_A_HEALTH_CONDITION)**: bursitis, adhesive capsulitis (frozen shoulder), carpal tunnel syndrome, plantar fasciitis, impingement syndrome, flat feet, acute joint injuries/sprains/dislocations/ligament tears (e.g. "ACL tear," "meniscus tear" as an acute injury phrasing rather than the degenerative sense) - these get NOT_A_HEALTH_CONDITION as injuries, not RMD. Bone fractures of any kind (including osteoporotic fractures, healed or not) are also NOT_A_HEALTH_CONDITION - acute trauma, not the chronic/degenerative disease Paper 1's RMD category targets. This localized/mechanical vs. systemic/degenerative line is a judgment call, not confirmed by Megan - worth flagging if it matters later.

6. **CVD**: standard cardiac/vascular diagnoses (MI/heart attack, CAD and its abbreviation, CHF and its abbreviation, cardiomyopathy including hypertrophic/dilated/obstructive variants, arrhythmias including AFib/bradycardia/PVCs/heart blocks, valve disease/disorder, stroke/CVA/TIA/lacunar stroke, DVT/PE/blood clots, atherosclerosis and coronary/carotid artery calcification/stenosis/blockage, congenital heart defects, structural findings like LV hypertrophy or atrial enlargement, aneurysms - aortic and other arterial, angina). "History of X" or "H/O X" or "Hx of X" phrasing for a CVD diagnosis still counts as CVD (the diagnosis is real even if not currently active). A diagnosis noted with a secondary "status post" procedure (e.g. "CAD s/p stent") keeps the diagnosis's CVD code since the diagnosis is still the dominant descriptor. Does NOT include: hypertension (pending), murmurs (usually benign), edema alone (too nonspecific), varicose veins (kept as OTHER), venous/vascular anomalies described as congenital/developmental and benign. Cardiac procedures themselves (CABG, stent placement, pacemaker, cardiac catheterization, angioplasty, carotid stenting, cardiac surgery generally) are NOT_A_HEALTH_CONDITION - they are interventions, not diagnoses, even though they imply an underlying CVD diagnosis; only code the diagnosis as CVD when the diagnosis itself is actually named in the term, not just implied by the procedure.

7. **T2D**: diabetes mentioned without Type 1/gestational/juvenile qualifier defaults to T2D (reasonable given adult melanoma trial population), including plain "diabetes," "history of diabetes," and abbreviations like DM, DM2, DM II, "insulin dependent diabetes" (without a Type 1 qualifier - insulin dependence alone doesn't imply Type 1 in an adult population, since many Type 2 patients are insulin-dependent). Diabetes complications (diabetic neuropathy, diabetic retinopathy, diabetic dyslipidemia) count as T2D since they imply the underlying diagnosis. Type 1 diabetes explicitly excluded. Gestational diabetes excluded (different pathophysiology, resolves postpartum, distinct from adult-onset T2D). Prediabetes-type language (impaired fasting glucose, glucose intolerance, elevated/high glucose alone, "borderline diabetes," "pre-diabetes," "glucose intolerance/diabetes" when ambiguous about whether it progressed to an actual diagnosis) does NOT count - these are lab findings/risk states, not a diabetes diagnosis, UNLESS explicitly qualified as "DM Type II" or similar.

8. **COPD**: explicit COPD/emphysema (including "centrilobular emphysema")/chronic bronchitis language. Bronchiectasis, asthma (including exercise-induced), and other chronic lung conditions do NOT count (distinct diagnoses).

9. **Lab values, vital signs, and isolated findings** (ALT/AST, alkaline phosphatase, bilirubin, creatinine, hemoglobin/hematocrit, electrolytes, blood counts, edema, murmurs, EF percentages, hearing loss/impairment, vision loss/changes, rashes, etc.) are OTHER_CONDITION_NOT_TRACKED, not diagnoses - this includes when phrased as "history of increased X" for a lab value.

10. **Condition + procedure combos** (e.g. "cholecystectomy due to cholelithiasis," "fibroids and subsequent hysterectomy," "cholecystitis (gallbladder) surgery," "gastrectomy for stomach cancer") get NOT_A_HEALTH_CONDITION when the procedure is the dominant descriptor of the term, regardless of how significant the implied underlying diagnosis is - this includes major/malignant conditions like "gastrectomy for stomach cancer" where the procedure noun leads the term. This also applies to any term that is fundamentally a bare procedure/intervention noun ("X excision," "X surgery," "X removal," "X repair," "X biopsy," "X resection," "CABG," "coronary artery bypass graft," "Mohs procedure/surgery," even when a diagnosis name like "melanoma" or "basal cell carcinoma" appears as a modifier within the procedure name, e.g. "melanoma excision," "Mohs surgery, neck basal cell CA") - these get NOT_A_HEALTH_CONDITION since the term names the intervention, not the diagnosis itself. Exception: when a real diagnosis is named as the grammatical subject with a status-post or "with" note attached rather than being wholly subordinated to a procedure name (e.g. "cervical cancer with hysterectomy," "CAD s/p stent," "malignant melanoma of left foot," "history of invasive breast cancer") - these keep the diagnosis's own code (typically O for non-tracked cancers, C for CVD diagnoses) since the diagnosis is still the dominant descriptor. Judgment call on where "dominant descriptor" tips from diagnosis to procedure; generally: if the term's grammatical head/first noun is a body-part-plus-diagnosis phrase, code the diagnosis; if the term's grammatical head is a procedure name, code N.

11. **Allergies and vaccine/drug reactions** (e.g. "allergic to penicillin," "allergy to IV contrast," "cat allergy," "dairy allergy," "environmental allergies," named-drug allergies of any kind) are NOT_A_HEALTH_CONDITION, consistent with the existing pattern for allergy entries already in the overrides file. Note: allergic rhinitis / hay fever / seasonal allergies as a standing diagnosis are also coded N in this dataset (matches prior precedent already in the file), distinct from OTHER_CONDITION_NOT_TRACKED conditions like atopic dermatitis or asthma.

12. **Family history terms** (e.g. "family history colon cancer-lynch," "FH: migraine headache") are NOT_A_HEALTH_CONDITION - they describe a relative's condition, not the patient's own medical history, so they don't belong in any of the four tracked comorbidity categories or as the patient's own OTHER_CONDITION.

13. **Melanoma/skin cancer diagnoses** (malignant melanoma, melanoma in situ, basal cell carcinoma, squamous cell carcinoma, and all site-specific variants) are consistently coded OTHER_CONDITION_NOT_TRACKED when the term names the diagnosis itself, and NOT_A_HEALTH_CONDITION when the term names a procedure on it (excision, resection, removal, wide local excision, Mohs, biopsy). This is a very high-frequency term family in this dataset (expected, given this is a melanoma trial) - apply the same diagnosis-vs-procedure split consistently.
