## Tasks finished today

**1. Decide which health condition categories to sort patients into**

"Comorbidity" means a health condition a patient has in addition to their
cancer, like diabetes or heart disease. We decided to use four categories,
based on Paper 1:

- Cardiovascular disease (CVD) - heart and blood vessel problems
- Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) - long-term lung damage that
  makes breathing hard
- Type 2 diabetes (T2D) - the common adult-onset form of diabetes
- Rheumatic and musculoskeletal disorders (RMD) - joint, bone, and
  connective tissue conditions like arthritis

We chose this smaller list instead of the full Charlson Comorbidity Index
(CCI) - a standard 17-category scoring system for measuring how much
chronic illness a patient has - because our data is written in plain text,
not official medical codes. A smaller list is easier to sort correctly.

**2. Confirm the patient ID number is still in our private files**

Patient ID number, called PATNO in our files, is the unique number
identifying each patient. We checked that this number is present in both
files, and confirmed it never gets shared with any AI tool or uploaded
anywhere outside our private local folder.

## Problems we found and worked through

**Problem 1: A duplicate column.**
The main file had two columns both named "ARM" (which arm, or treatment
group, a patient was in). We checked and confirmed both columns had exactly
the same information, so we removed the extra copy.

**Problem 2: Extra patient ID numbers that don't belong to the trial.**
The medical history file had 142 patient ID numbers that don't match any
of the 1,301 trial patients. We checked carefully to rule out a formatting
mistake (like a number being written differently in two places) - that
wasn't the cause. Our best guess: these may be patients who were screened
for the trial but never actually joined it, so they have medical history
on file but no trial results. This doesn't cause a problem for our
analysis, since these extra IDs will automatically be left out when we
combine the two files later. We're asking the mentor to confirm this
guess is correct.

**Problem 3: 18 patient ID numbers in an unusual format.**
Most patient ID numbers are 6 characters long. 18 of them are 18
characters long instead - clearly a different format. We don't know why
yet. It's a very small number of rows, so it likely won't affect the
results either way, but we're flagging it for the mentor.

**Problem 4: Some medical history entries don't say if the condition is
ongoing.**
38 entries are missing the ongoing-or-not marker (MHONGO). Of those, 21
are just blank rows (nothing to worry about), but 17 have a real medical
history entry with no ongoing/not-ongoing marker. For now we're assuming
these count as ongoing, since we can't rule it out, but we're checking
this assumption with the mentor.
