## Resolution (July 8, 2026)

Megan replied without giving a strict per-term ruling. She said she doesn't recognize the RMD abbreviation, was skeptical that something like chronic ankle pain counts as a real comorbidity, thought morbid obesity seemed like an important health condition, asked for existing research on the evidence threshold other studies use, and said the point of this classification step was to avoid item-by-item judgment calls in the first place.

Based on that plus a literature check on how standard comorbidity indices (Charlson, Elixhauser) actually treat these terms, all 281 W-tagged entries were resolved:
- Hypertension (all variants) -> excluded. Standard CCI does not score hypertension at all.
- High cholesterol/hyperlipidemia/hypertriglyceridemia (all variants) -> excluded. Also not part of standard CCI.
- Obesity/overweight/metabolic syndrome -> kept, but tagged as "other condition, tracked" rather than folded into CVD. This matches how the literature handles it: obesity is excluded from CCI itself and analyzed as a separate variable in cancer/immunotherapy studies, not merged into a cardiovascular score.
- Vague musculoskeletal pain terms with no diagnosis attached (arthralgia, joint pain, back pain, etc.) -> excluded, consistent with Megan's skepticism about chronic ankle pain and how CCI's rheumatic category requires an actual diagnosis.
- Two "aneurysm" entries that were sitting in this pending list by mistake were corrected to CVD, since an aneurysm is an actual vascular diagnosis, not a risk factor.

Full writeup with citations for the CCI claims is in classification/classification_methodology.md.
