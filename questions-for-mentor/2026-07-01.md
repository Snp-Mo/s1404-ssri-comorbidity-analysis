# Questions for Mentor — July 1, 2026

Five questions from today, in order of importance. Each one explains why
we're asking and what the answer would help us decide.

## 1. Is our plan for grouping health conditions okay? (most important)

**What we're asking:** We want to sort patients' health conditions into
four groups: cardiovascular disease (CVD, heart and blood vessel
problems), chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD, long-term lung
damage), type 2 diabetes (T2D), and rheumatic and musculoskeletal
disorders (RMD, joint and bone conditions like arthritis). This is a
smaller list than the full Charlson Comorbidity Index (CCI), a standard
17-condition scoring system, but we think it's easier to sort correctly
since our data is plain text, not official medical codes. Does this plan
sound right, or should we use a different set of categories?

**Why we're asking:** This decision affects everything we do next. If we
start sorting the data one way and then have to switch plans, we'd have
to redo the work.

**What the answer helps with:** Confirms whether it's okay to trade some
completeness for something we can actually do accurately with the data
we have, or whether there's a better middle option.

## 2. Are the extra patient numbers in the medical history file expected?

**What we're asking:** We found 142 patient ID numbers in the medical
history file that don't match any of the 1,301 patients in the main
trial file. Our guess is that these are patients who were screened for
the trial but never actually joined it. Does that sound right? Is it
okay to just leave these patients out of our analysis?

**Why we're asking:** When two files that are supposed to describe the
same group of people don't quite match, it's worth double-checking
before trusting the numbers.

**What the answer helps with:** Confirms whether our guess is correct,
or tells us if something else is going on that we need to fix.

## 3. What might these unusually long patient ID numbers be?

**What we're asking:** Most patient ID numbers are 6 characters long. We
found 18 that are 18 characters long instead. Do you know what these
might be?

**Why we're asking:** It's a small number of rows and probably doesn't
matter much, but we want to flag it rather than ignore it.

**What the answer helps with:** Tells us whether to just leave these out
(which we're already doing, since they don't match any trial patient) or
look into them further.

## 4. What should we do with medical history entries that aren't health
conditions?

**What we're asking:** Some entries in the medical history file are
things like past surgeries or allergies, not ongoing health conditions.
Should we just skip these during sorting, or keep a separate "not a
health condition" category so it's clear we didn't miss them?

**Why we're asking:** This affects how we build our sorting process, and
whether it's easy to double-check later.

**What the answer helps with:** Gives us a clear rule to follow instead
of us guessing partway through the work.

## 5. How should we handle entries missing the "ongoing" marker?

**What we're asking:** 17 medical history entries have a real health
condition listed, but no marker saying whether it's ongoing or resolved.
Right now we're planning to assume these count as ongoing, since we
can't tell otherwise. Does that sound like a reasonable default?

**Why we're asking:** It's an assumption we're making, not something
confirmed by the data.

**What the answer helps with:** Confirms whether our default is fine or
whether there's a better way to handle it.
